About Me

I am a blind accessibility architect based in Chennai, India. I lost my sight at age 12, and since then I have navigated the digital world using screen readers, a Braille display, and keyboard-only interaction — every single day.

Over 16+ years, I have worked across accessibility auditing, strategy, team leadership, and training. My work spans web, mobile, and document accessibility for organisations in technology, banking, and financial services.

I don't simulate disability to test for it. I live it. That perspective shapes everything I do — from the audits I conduct to the teams I train.

If you'd like to know more about my work and the organisations I've partnered with, let's have a conversation.

What I Do

Accessibility Auditing

I evaluate websites, mobile apps, and documents against WCAG and other standards — using assistive technology as my primary tool, not just automated scanners.

Strategy & Compliance

I help organisations define their accessibility roadmap, align with regulatory requirements, and integrate accessibility into their development lifecycle.

Training & Mentorship

I train developers, designers, and QA teams on accessibility practices. I also help organisations set up internal accessibility champions programmes.

Regulatory Guidance

I work with global standards like WCAG, ADA, Section 508, and EN 301 549 — as well as India-specific regulations including RPwD Act 2016, GIGW 3.0, and the SEBI accessibility mandate.
