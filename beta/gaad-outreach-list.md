# AMASAMYA — GAAD 2026 Outreach List

People and organisations to soft-touch on or around GAAD 2026
(Thursday 21 May 2026). Mixed India + global, equal weight.

This is a working file. Strike rows that go nowhere; add rows for
contacts who reach out. Do not bulk-send — every row is a one-to-one
warm DM or email customised in your own voice.

## How to use this list

1. Pick five rows for **day 0** (GAAD Thursday). Send DM or email
   between 8 and 10 am IST. Customise the opening sentence to refer
   to something specific about the recipient.
2. Pick five more for **day plus 1** (Friday). Same approach.
3. Pick five more for **day plus 3** (Monday). Same.
4. Drop the rest. Cold reach-outs after the first three days are
   noise.

The point is not to reach everyone. The point is to have one or two
warm conversations with the right people.

## Tone for every message

- Plain, direct, no marketing language.
- Lead with one sentence that proves you read what they do.
- Mention that you are a screen-reader user yourself in the first or
  second sentence (this is the credibility signal that matters in
  this audience).
- One specific ask only. Either: would you take a look, would you
  share, or would you reply with one piece of feedback.
- One link. Either the GAAD blog post, the extension listing, or the
  GitHub repo — never all three in one DM.
- Sign-off with your full name, your link, and nothing else.

## How to find the right person at each org

For organisations on this list, the most useful contact is rarely the
generic info@ address. Try this order:

1. LinkedIn search: `[org name] accessibility`. Look for people with
   "Accessibility Lead", "Director of Accessibility", "Inclusive Design"
   in their headline. They are the ones who actually try tools.
2. Their public a11y blog post authors. Anyone publishing accessibility
   content publicly is the right person to reach.
3. Twitter / Mastodon a11y community. Many of the named contacts above
   post regularly with #a11y or #accessibility.
4. As a last resort, the contact form on the org website.

## Track replies in this same file

Add a "Replied?" column with `Y/N/Pending` once you start sending.

chase a second time without a real reason.

## What success looks like

If by end of week 2 (4 June 2026) you have:

- Three or more replies that include a specific piece of feedback,
- One DM from someone in the global list asking for a call,
- One Indian org sharing the launch post in their community,

then the GAAD launch worked. Anything beyond that is upside.

If you have only one or two non-specific replies (just "thanks" or
"will check it out"), that is also a valid outcome — keep posting,
keep auditing, the compounding is slow but real.
